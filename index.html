"The Great Gatsby" in three sentences

Beginning

Jay Gatsby devotes his life to rekindling his relationship with Daisy Buchanan, a woman he loved and lost many years ago.

Middle

Though he becomes everything Daisy is looking for, circumstances and Daisy's own trepidations keep them apart.

End

Their realities were divorced from the idea of their perfect love. Events are set in motion that lead to a tragic ending.
